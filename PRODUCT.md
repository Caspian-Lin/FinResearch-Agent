# Product

## Register

product

## Users

FinResearch Agent is used by AI engineers, quantitative researchers, fintech builders,
and advanced students who need a repeatable way to turn investment hypotheses into
auditable research workflows. They work in an authenticated dashboard, often comparing
market data, watchlists, backtest results, and data-quality signals under time pressure.

Their job is not to place trades. Their job is to inspect assumptions, reproduce a
research run, understand data limitations, and communicate findings with enough caveat
and traceability that another person can review the work.

## Product Purpose

FinResearch Agent turns natural-language investment hypotheses into reproducible
research workflows: data retrieval, factor construction, backtesting, risk evaluation,
benchmark comparison, and research memo generation.

Success means a user can see what data was used, what window and universe were tested,
which parameters drove the result, how the result changes after costs, and where the
limitations are. The product must position itself as research automation and
reproducibility infrastructure, not as an automated trading system or profit engine.

## Brand Personality

The product should feel precise, calm, and accountable.

The voice is plainspoken and methodical. It should communicate institutional confidence
without financial grandstanding: clear labels, visible caveats, restrained visual
emphasis, and no language that implies guaranteed alpha, buy/sell advice, or automated
execution.

## Anti-references

Avoid anything that makes the product feel like a brokerage, signal-selling service,
casino, or performance-marketing fintech page.

Do not use hype language about beating the market, guaranteed returns, "winning"
strategies, or urgent calls to act. Avoid decorative terminal cosplay, neon trading
dashboards, glossy crypto-exchange aesthetics, purple-blue gradient SaaS tropes,
glassmorphism, and oversized marketing hero metrics.

## Design Principles

1. **Methodology before performance.** Lead with data windows, universes, assumptions,
   baselines, and cost sensitivity before celebrating metrics.
2. **Risk is part of the interface.** Disclaimers, limitations, quality warnings, and
   failed states should be visible where decisions are made.
3. **Dense when useful, quiet by default.** The dashboard can carry tables, controls,
   and charts, but ornament should not compete with research tasks.
4. **Bilingual without layout fragility.** English and Simplified Chinese labels must
   fit in controls, navigation, cards, and tables without truncation that hides meaning.
5. **Reproducibility is a product feature.** Surfaces should make parameters, sources,
   run status, and timestamps easy to recover.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for product UI. Body text and placeholders need at least 4.5:1
contrast; large text needs at least 3:1. Controls must have visible focus states and
keyboard-accessible flows.

Charts must not rely on color alone: pair color with labels, legends, line style, or
position. Motion should be state-driven, brief, and respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
Financial warnings and limitations should be available in both English and Simplified
Chinese.
